What is a brush pen?

A brush pen is similar to the nib pen, with the main difference being that a brush pen has ink inside its body while a nib pen must be dipped in ink every so often to continue writing. A brush pen also has a soft, flexible tip compared the the hard metal tip of the nib pen. Its tip can be used to create thin strokes while applying pressure to it allows the strokes to thicken.

How does it work?

The traditional rule is that all upstrokes are thin, applying little pressure whilst all downstrokes are thick applying certain amounts of pressure depending on how think you would want it to be.

Reflection

At first, calligraphy was something I thought I could never be able to do. The way I write isn't careful or elegant at all and lacked control, which is what I thought was needed to do calligraphy. However, I saw Froi writing one day and thought to myself I wanna learn because it not only looked cool, but also challenging. When I started I was somehow hooked, often I'd practice strokes while daydreaming using a pencil or even just pretend-writing onto thin air. I knew that I wanted to have calligraphy be the theme of this project as soon as it was announced because I wanted to practice and also let out my creativity (if you could call it that) in calligraphy. I do believe I improved where I can write decently. I only regret getting too lazy and falling behind schedule and even letting my partner down in the process (if I did). Nevertheless, this project is certainly a stepping stone towards developing my own calligraphy. I sure hope I can show people some day! :))
